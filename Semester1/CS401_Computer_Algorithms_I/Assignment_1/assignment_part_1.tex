\problem{1a}

\begin{question}
    True or false? In every instance of the Stable Matching Problem, there is a 
    stable matching containing a pair \textit{(m,w)} such that \textit{m} is 
    ranked first on the preference list of \textit{w} and \textit{w} is ranked 
    first on the preference list of \textit{m}.
    $ $\newline
\end{question}
\begin{solution}
    Let us approach this problem by considering a group containing 2 men 
    \textit{m\textsubscript{1}} \& \textit{m\textsubscript{2}} and 2 women
    \textit{w\textsubscript{1}} \& \textit{w\textsubscript{2}}. 
    $ $\newline
    We will first see whether the above claim is possible to reproduce using 
    a specific set of preferences and then disprove it with another altered 
    set of preferences.
    $ $\newline
    Let the preferences of \textit{m\textsubscript{1}} and 
    \textit{m\textsubscript{2}} be as follows: 
    \begin{center}
        \begin{tabular}{ | l | c | r | }
            \hline
            & 1\textsuperscript{st} & 2\textsuperscript{nd} \\ \hline
            m\textsubscript{1} & w\textsubscript{1} & w\textsubscript{2} \\ \hline
            m\textsubscript{2} & w\textsubscript{1} & w\textsubscript{2} \\
            \hline
        \end{tabular}
    \end{center}
\end{solution}
